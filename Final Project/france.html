France

Art

It is hard to think of a country where art is more essential to the society as a whole than France. Throughout the Middle Ages, French art produced some of the greatest examples of Romanesque and Gothic architecture and art in history, giving France an exceptional legacy. The court painter Jean Clouet proudly embodied the French Renaissance era, and by the middle of the 17th century, Nicolas Poussin had taken on the role as the founding father of the French classical heritage. For many, the enchanting frivolity of Rococo marked the beginning of completely French art. However, the age of Enlightenment and the introduction of Neoclassicism, Romanticism, and Realism to the world's artists of a certain level eventually replaced it.

Local Cruisine

The cuisine of France is among the most delicious and well-respected in the world. Savoring regional cuisine is a way of life for residents, and one of the biggest draws for tourists to the nation is its French cuisine. French cuisine is known for its flavor and refinement, but it also offers an incredible variety of regional specialties reflecting each region's unique products, history, and culture. The amazing variety of French recipes can be due to a variety of factors, including the country's climate, fish species found in the sea, local cheeses and wines, farming and animals, and even the influence of neighboring nations.
